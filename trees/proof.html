trees

i am walking to the [[station]]. there is a [[silver gum tree]] in the front yard of a townhouse. i want to [[stop and stare]].i am worried that i will miss the [[train]], even though i left home with plenty of time to spare. time is not something i have befriended. i seldom pay it attention, so it passes without my notice. i am worried that time speeds up when i am not looking.the train takes me to the city. there are buildings, steely blue. a silver gum would not stay silver for long. i wonder how long the silver would hold.it is not true silver. it glints with a dull green and streaks of grey and brown. the branches droop under the weight of their gumnuts. good sized gumnuts. ones i would collect as a child and stuff in my pockets as treasure. but it does not drop its gumnuts lightly. it has long, elegant leaves, tapering to delicately curved points. it is a hushed, whispery tree. it is a wise tree; a tree of power. i stop and stare. it is a witchy tree: it shimmers. beauty. ether. magic. i stare and stare. i miss my train. and another. i lose feeling in my feet. i stare so long, the rest of the world drops away. time does not exist. i can only see the tree. i only want to see the tree. i stop blinking. i cannot lose sight of the tree at all. i am bound to the tree. it will not let me go. i do not want it to.
